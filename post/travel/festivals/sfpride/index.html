Sfpride

4 mins

San Francisco Pride, SF

To be honest this is one festival we were not prepared for or rather we did not know off. We landed in SF on 30th June to be informed by our friend Aditi(who we were to stay with) that we need to reach home soon as the PRIDE parade starts at 10:30 AM! We were surprised! It was still early in the day when we had landed from New Jersey and we took a cab from the airport to reach her place. Thankfully all the roads blockages were still not in place by then and we managed to reach soonish! This was our first PRIDE parade and we were very excited to be able to attend it in SF üòÑ

What usually happens(in short)?

The parade consists of hundreds of contingents from various groups and organizations. Many San Francisco companies have a contingent, either chaptered by LGBT employees of the company or by the company as a community outreach to show support of LGBT causes or purely as a PR move. Hundreds of thousands of spectators line the parade route along Market Street.

When did we attend it?

30th June, 2019. Sunday

The San Francisco Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender Pride Celebration usually known as San Francisco Pride, is a parade and festival held at the end of June each year in San Francisco, California, to celebrate the lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) people and their allies.

Any dress code?

Get your most colorful clothes out. The more colors the better. Do not wear monotone! Make sure you have a comfortable pair of shoes as a lot of walking will be involved. Have a tote tag with you for the goodies and water as it is hot!

You will also find colorful stuff in some street stalls or you might get lucky and be handed over stuff from one of the contingents üòÅ.

How to enjoy PRIDE parade to the fullest?

The route is usually west along San Francisco’s Market Street, from Steuart Street to 8th Street and it runs from 10:30 am until almost 3:00 pm. Participants line up off the parade route in advance of the start of the parade.. So first things first, reach Market St.

As soon as you reach you will notice LOTS of dressed up people standing behind barricades on either side with Parade in its full glory marching in the center. Find yourself a good spot to see the parade and capture some photos.

To enjoy this to the fullest you have to go with an open mind, especially if it’s your first PRIDE parade. Acceptance is probably all that you need to have for all communities. The message “Love is Love” kinds makes sense when you see the parade. So find a spot and join the colors.

A lot of contingents will be giving out goodies and we saw so many who were there for the goodies üòÇ. Make sure you grab the colorful necklaces so that you can wear them and add the glow to your getup. You will get literally anything: stickers, badges, flags, hand-fans, some really expensive gifts from bigger brands like Sephora, some food coupons. The idea is to spread the awareness about the communities and help people accept. Although SF is already way ahead of its time when it comes to acceptance but its about continuing the tradition that started here once I guess.

Get your kids! We saw so many families picnicking along side the barricades. It is such a brilliant way to let your kids understand love and not grow up phobic to things that the “society” defines as “normal”.

Smile! There will be people dancing and singing in each contingents. They will be looking at you as well. The least you can do is smile. There will be folks coming for a high five, give them one!

Get that camera ready! This is one of the most colorful parades you can see. SO make sure you have a good spot. And thankfully if anyone in the contingent sees the camera they will happily pose for you! Shoot them, they want you to! You are not invading their privacy, you are helping spread a message.

The restaurants are all open, so you can go and grab food when you get tired.

The parade ends around the Civic center which has it’s own party going on. There is a stage with people dancing around it and a lot of stalls for food, drinks and accessories. You can join the dance party near the stage, maybe get your face painted as well. We choose to sit down and watch people pass by, took some photos and talk to those who seemed interested. It is really a very pleasant experience.

How to move around during PRIDE?

Walk or take the subway. The entire Market street is blocked and because of it all other streets are jam packed as well.
