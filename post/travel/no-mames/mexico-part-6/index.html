Mexico Part 6

by Karan

Part of the series : Island Hopping

2 mins

    • The Island to the North

It was with reluctance that we left Valladolid. But we had other things on our agenda that needed to be looked into. So we packed our bags and walked the pastel coloured lanes of the town we had so fallen in love with. We went to our faithful ADO bus station and booked a ticket to Isla Holbox.

The Island to the North

Holbox was an island in Quintana Roo, at the northern eastern tip of the Yucatan peninsula. The bus took us through villages and small towns and finally dropped us at Chiquila, where we bought ourselves tickets for the ferry to the island. Two services operate out of the port and there is a boat every 30 minutes. The fares are reasonable enough to not stick in my memory.

At the island we walked the distance to our AirBnB. The island does not have any motor vehicles but the luxury seekers have created a demand for golf carts. I find the concept rather ridiculous. Bicycles work just as well but then who wants the physical effort, eh?

The AirBnB was another simple one with a kitchenette and a attached bathroom. There was an air conditioner as well and we absolutely needed it out here. The days were sultry and the heat was quite oppressive.
